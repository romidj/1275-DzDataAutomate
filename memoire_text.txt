Introduction
The emergence of artificial intelligence and automated machine learning as mainstream analytical
tools has fundamentally transformed the way organizations extract value from data. What once
required the sustained effort of a team of specialized engineers can today, in principle, be accom-
plished in a matter of minutes by a well-designed software system. Y et this transformation has
remained, in practice, largely confined to large enterprises with the technical capacity and finan-
cial resources to deploy and operate these tools. For the vast majority of organizations : the small
business owner managing a boutique in Algiers, the NGO program officer tracking field interven-
tions in Batna, the masterвҖҷs student analyzing survey results at a regional university , the promise
of data-driven decision-making remains entirely out of reach.
This gap is not a technical problem. The algorithms exist, the computing infrastructure is accessible,
and the data is being collected. The gap is architectural: existing platforms have been designed by
data scientists, for data scientists, and the assumptions embedded in their interfaces, their pricing
structures, and their deployment models systematically exclude the non-technical majority. No
amount of incremental improvement to these platforms will bridge that gap, because the gap is not
in the capability of the tools but in the design philosophy underlying them.
The present project proposes a response to this gap in the specific context of the Algerian market.
DzDataAutomate is an intelligent web platform that automates the complete data science workflow
: from raw file ingestion through cleaning, visualization, and predictive analysis , within an inter-
face designed from the ground up for users without any data science training. The user does not
configure models, does not select algorithms, and does not interpret statistical outputs. They state
their professional context and their analytical goal in plain language, upload their data file, and the
platform guides them through a structured five-stage pipeline that concludes with a downloadable
report and a reproducible Python script. Every design decision in the platform reflects a single gov-
erning principle: the analytical capability of an enterprise system, delivered through an experience
accessible to anyone.
The project is developed within the framework of Ministerial Order 1275, which establishes the
DiplГҙme-Startup pathway at Algerian universities, and is incubated at ESTIN under the supervision
of Dr. Amara Karima and Dr. Benslimane Salim. It is carried out by a three-member founding team
specializing in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, and represents both an academic deliverable
and the first operational version of a startup intended for commercial launch.
This memoir is organized into six chapters. Chapter 1 presents the project idea, the proposed values,
the team, the objectives, and the implementation timeline. Chapter 2 analyzes the innovative di-
mensions of the platform across four domains: process automation, intent-driven interaction, new
customer segments, and privacy-preserving architecture. Chapter 3 conducts a strategic market
analysis covering the competitive landscape, barriers to entry, identified market gaps, and the plat-
formвҖҷs competitive positioning. Chapter 4 describes the production and organization plan, includ-
ing the service delivery pipeline, infrastructure, human resources, and key partnerships. Chapter
5 presents the financial plan across four components: costs and charges, projected revenue, ex-
pected income statement, and treasury plan. Chapter 6 presents the experimental prototype through
a screen-by-screen description of the platformвҖҷs current implementation, demonstrating the cor-
respondence between the design principles articulated in the earlier chapters and their concrete
realization in a navigable web application.

1.1 The Project Idea (Proposed Solution)
Our project operates in the domain of digital services and modern applications, specifically the
automation of data analysis through artificial intelligence.
The idea originated from a straightforward but revealing observation: a large number of Algerian
small and medium businesses, students, and researchers collect data on a daily basis: sales records,
survey results, inventory logs, customer data, yet never manage to extract real value from it. Existing
analysis tools such as Google AutoML, DataRobot, or Python-based environments require advanced
technical skills that most of these users simply do not have. At the same time, hiring a professional
data analyst represents a cost that is out of reach for a small business or an independent researcher.
These users find themselves in a paradoxical situation: they have the data, but not the means to
understand it.
In response to this gap, our project proposes an intelligent web platform called DzDataAutomate,
accessible from any browser with no installation required. The concept works as follows: the user
uploads their data file in CSV or Excel format, states their objective in plain terms, and the platform
automatically handles data cleaning, generates relevant visualizations, and executes predictive or
descriptive analyses tailored to the userвҖҷs declared need. Every step of the process is presented in
plain language with no technical jargon, and the session concludes with a downloadable report and
a documented Python script that allows the user to reproduce or extend the analysis independently.
Development relies on open-source technologiesНҫ primarily Python, Streamlit, Pandas, Scikit-learn,
and Plotlywhich, keeps development costs at a minimum while guaranteeing high performance.
1.2 Proposed Values
The values our platform delivers to its users are organized around the following dimensions:
Modernity: There is currently no data analysis automation platform designed specifically for the
Algerian market, available in for this specific community, and adapted to the realities of local small
structures. Our solution addresses an entirely new need for which no similar local offering previ-
ously existed.
Performance: The platform is capable of processing, cleaning, and visualizing a standard dataset in
a really short amount of time. Analysis models are selected automatically by the system according
to best-performance criteria, delivering reliable results without any intervention from the user.
Flexibility: The user has access to two cleaning modes (automatic or step-by-step) and can choose
from the suggested analyses the ones that precisely match their need. The platform adapts to the
type of data provided and the intent profile declared by the user at the beginning of each session.
Task accomplishment: A boutique owner, an NGO manager, or a masterвҖҷs student can go from a
raw disorganized file to a complete downloadable analysis report without writing a single line of
code and without any prior training in data science.
User-centered design: Every screen of the platform is designed to guide the user step by step,
asking questions in natural language rather than asking them to configure technical parameters.
The experience feels more like a guided conversation than a traditional analysis software interface.
Cost reduction: Access to the platform is free for basic use, making it accessible to users who
could not afford to hire a data analyst or purchase a professional software license. Infrastructure
costs are minimized through the use of open-source cloud services.
Risk reduction: User data is never permanently stored on our servers. Only the statistical pro-
file of the dataset is transmitted to the AI model for generating analysis suggestions, ensuring the
confidentiality of sensitive business information throughout the entire session.
Accessibility: The platform is accessible from any device with a web browser, with no installation,
no mandatory subscription, and no technical prerequisite. It explicitly targets users who have never
had access to data analysis tools before.
Ease of use: The interface follows a clear step-by-step progression: intent capture, upload, cleaning,

2.1 Nature of Innovation
The platform sits at the intersection of technological innovation and market innovation it introduces
no fundamentally new ML algorithm, but radically transforms how data science is accessed and
delivered, which is where the real innovation lies.
2.2 Innovation Domains
2.2.1 New Processes: Automating the Data Science Workflow
The proposed platform fundamentally redefines the data science workflow by replacing a tradition-
ally complex, multi-day manual process with a fully automated four-stage pipeline executable in a
matter of minutes. In conventional practice, extracting actionable insights from raw data requires
the sequential intervention of a trained data scientist across several distinct phases: exploratory data
analysis, data cleaning and preprocessing, feature engineering, model selection and hyperparameter
tuning, training, evaluation, and finally the interpretation and communication of results. Each of
these phases demands both domain expertise and significant time investment, rendering the process
inaccessible to the vast majority of potential users.
The platform addresses this barrier through a rule-based automation engine that handles each phase
without requiring user intervention. At Stage 1, the system performs an immediate silent scan of the
uploaded dataset, producing a structured data passport that summarizes row count, column types,
missing value distribution, and detected categorical and temporal features. At Stage 2, the cleaning
pipeline applies a deterministic sequence of operations: duplicate removal, missing value imputa-
tion using median and mode strategies, outlier flagging based on standard deviation thresholds, date
format standardization, and near-empty column elimination. This process can be executed either
automatically in a single click or guided step by step for users who wish to retain control. At Stage
4, the system automatically detects the analytical task type based on the target column characteris-
tics, selects and trains the most appropriate model from a predefined set of scikit-learn estimators,
evaluates performance using relevant metrics, and presents results through interactive visualizations
accompanied by plain-language summaries.
The result is a complete analytical workflow, from raw CSV to predictive model and interpreted
dashboard, that a non-technical user can execute in under five minutes, without writing a single line
of code or possessing any knowledge of statistics or machine learning. This represents a substan-
tial increase in operational efficiency and democratizes access to data-driven decision-making for
organizations that cannot afford dedicated data science teams.
2.2.2 New Functionalities: Intent-Driven Analysis via AI Suggestion Cards
A central functional innovation of the platform is its intent-driven interaction model, which inverts
the conventional relationship between the user and the analytical tool. In existing platforms, in-
cluding spreadsheet-based tools, business intelligence dashboards, and AutoML solutions, the user
is expected to arrive with a predefined analytical objective and sufficient technical literacy to trans-
late that objective into a query, a chart configuration, or a model specification. This prerequisite
excludes the majority of potential users, particularly those in small and medium-sized enterprises
who possess domain knowledge but lack data science training.
The proposed platform resolves this by capturing user intent before any data is processed. At Stage
0, the user is presented with two simple visual card selections: the type of data they are working
with, such as sales, customers, operations, research, and similar categories, and the analytical goal
they wish to achieve, such as understanding past performance, predicting future values, grouping
similar records, or cleaning data only. This intent profile is stored and carried through all subsequent
stages, allowing the system to tailor its behavior, including cleaning strategy, model selection, and

output format, to the declared objective.
Following data ingestion and automated cleaning at Stages 1 and 2, the platform executes Stage 3,
the AI layer, which constitutes the most distinctive functional contribution of the system. A single
call to a locally deployed large language model via Ollama transmits only the intent profile and
a compact data profile containing column names, inferred types, and descriptive statistics. The
model processes this information and returns a structured JSON response containing three to five
analytical suggestions expressed in plain business language, such as вҖңWhich product generated the
most revenue last quarter?вҖқ, вҖңPredict next monthвҖҷs sales based on historical trendsвҖқ, or вҖңIdentify
which customer segments are most valuable.вҖқ Each suggestion is tagged with a reference to a pre-
built analysis module in the backend, so that when the user selects a card, the system immediately
routes the cleaned dataset to the appropriate scikit-learn pipeline and produces results without any
further input.
This architecture achieves two simultaneous objectives. First, it eliminates the knowledge barrier
that prevents non-technical users from engaging with analytical tools, by replacing open-ended
queries with contextually generated, actionable suggestions. Second, it ensures that the AI compo-
nent enhances rather than replaces the deterministic pipeline: if the language model is unavailable
or produces an unexpected response, the system falls back gracefully to a default set of suggestions
derived from the detected data types and the intent profile alone. The analytical engine continues
to function correctly regardless of the AI layerвҖҷs availability, which is a critical reliability property
for a demonstration environment.
2.2.3 New Customers: Reaching the Non-Technical Majority
The competitive landscape of automated machine learning and business intelligence is currently
dominated by platforms designed for technically sophisticated users operating within large organi-
zational structures. Google V ertex AI, DataRobot, H2O.ai, and Microsoft Azure Machine Learning
all require users to possess working knowledge of data preprocessing concepts, model evaluation
metrics, and cloud infrastructure management. Their pricing models, which typically operate on
enterprise subscription or pay-per-compute bases, further restrict access to organizations with ded-
icated technology budgets. As a consequence, the substantial segment of the market composed
of small and medium-sized enterprises, independent professionals, students, researchers, and non-
governmental organizations has been systematically excluded from the benefits of automated data
analysis.
This exclusion is particularly pronounced in the Algerian market, where the digital transformation
of small businesses remains at an early stage, dedicated data science teams are rare outside of large
public institutions and multinational subsidiaries, and awareness of AutoML tools is limited. Field
observations and the strategic analysis conducted as part of this project indicate that many Algerian
SME operators collect transactional and operational data, including sales records, inventory logs,
and customer registers, but lack the means to extract analytical value from it. The cost of engag-
ing an external data consultant, where available, represents a significant barrier. Cloud-based AI
solutions are further constrained by data sovereignty concerns, inconsistent internet connectivity in
certain regions, and a general reluctance to entrust operational data to foreign providers.
The proposed platform is designed from the ground up to serve this underserved segment. Every
design decision, including the intent-capture card interface at Stage 0, the plain-language suggestion
cards at Stage 3, the auto-mode cleaning pipeline at Stage 2, the natural-language result summaries
at Stage 4, and the local deployment model enabled by Ollama, reflects a deliberate prioritization
of accessibility over configurability. The target user is not a data scientist who wants fine-grained
control over hyperparametersНҫ it is a boutique owner who wants to know which product to reorder,
a student who wants to predict exam outcomes from study habit data, or an NGO program manager
who wants to identify which intervention regions show the greatest need. By serving these users

2.2.4 New Offers: A Privacy-Preserving, Offline-Compatible Analysis Platform
A key innovation of the proposed platform lies in its ability to provide AI-assisted data analysis
while preserving user privacy. To achieve this objective, the system integrates a locally deployed
large language model through Ollama, enabling natural language interactions without requiring the
transmission of sensitive information to external cloud services.
The AI layer, corresponding to Stage 3 of the proposed architecture, operates exclusively on a data
profile generated from the uploaded dataset rather than on the raw data itself. This profile contains
only metadata and aggregated information: column names, data types, descriptive statistics, and
dataset structure. Under no circumstances are the original records or confidential business data
exposed to the language model.
This design choice directly addresses one of the major barriers identified during our strategic analy-
sis of the Algerian market: data sharing hesitancy and skepticism toward cloud-based AI solutions.
Many organizations, particularly small and medium-sized enterprises, remain reluctant to adopt in-
telligent systems that require transferring operational data to third-party providers. By ensuring
that the analytical process remains local, the platform enhances trust and encourages the adoption
of data-driven practices.
Furthermore, the use of Ollama allows the language model to run entirely on the userвҖҷs machine,
eliminating dependency on external APIs and reducing operational costs. The platformвҖҷs core func-
tionalities: data ingestion, preprocessing, automated analysis, and predictive modeling can therefore
be executed in environments with limited internet connectivity or strict data governance require-
ments.
This privacy-preserving and offline-compatible architecture constitutes a significant differentiat-
ing factor compared with conventional cloud-centric analytical platforms. It demonstrates that ad-
vanced AI capabilities can be delivered while respecting data sovereignty, confidentiality, and the
practical constraints faced by organizations operating in emerging markets.

3.1 Identification of Competitors
The competitive landscape surrounding AI-powered data analysis platforms can be segmented into
two distinct categories: established international solutions operating at enterprise or professional
scale, and emerging or nascent national offerings within the Algerian market.
3.1.1 International Competitors
DataRobot is an enterprise-grade AutoML platform that automates the end-to-end machine learn-
ing lifecycle, from data ingestion to model deployment and monitoring. It offers sophisticated
model explainability features and supports a wide range of supervised learning tasks. Its target
market is primarily large enterprises with dedicated data science teams seeking to accelerate model
development rather than replace technical expertise entirely.
Google Vertex AI is Google CloudвҖҷs unified machine learning platform, integrating AutoML capa-
bilities with custom model training, pipeline orchestration, and deployment infrastructure. It pro-
vides access to state-of-the-art foundation models and is tightly coupled with the broader Google
Cloud ecosystem. Its usage presupposes familiarity with cloud architecture and machine learning
concepts.
Microsoft Power BI is a business intelligence and data visualization platform that enables users to
connect to diverse data sources, build interactive dashboards, and share reports across organizations.
While accessible to non-technical business users for visualization purposes, it does not provide
automated machine learning or predictive modeling capabilities in its standard configuration, and
its advanced AI features require integration with Azure Machine Learning.
KNIME Analytics Platform is an open-source data science workbench that enables users to con-
struct analytical workflows through a visual node-based interface. It supports a broad range of data
processing, machine learning, and integration tasks. Although it lowers the programming barrier
relative to scripting environments, it still requires a conceptual understanding of data science work-
flows and is primarily adopted by analysts and researchers rather than general business users.
RapidMiner is a data science platform offering visual workflow design, AutoML, and model de-
ployment capabilities. Like KNIME, it targets data analysts and citizen data scientists within or-
ganizations, and its enterprise licensing model places it beyond the reach of small businesses and
individual users.
Orange Data Mining is an open-source visual programming environment for data analysis and ma-
chine learning, developed by the University of Ljubljana. It is widely used in academic settings and
offers an accessible interface for educational purposes, though it lacks the integration, scalability,
and natural language interaction capabilities expected in a production business context.
3.1.2 National Competitors
At the time of this analysis, no documented Algerian startup or established domestic software pub-
lisher offers a platform functionally comparable to the proposed solution. Data analysis services in
Algeria are primarily delivered through consultancy arrangements, wherein organizations engage

4.1 The Service Delivery Process
Since our project is a digital platform rather than a physical product, the production process cor-
responds to the sequence of operations the system performs to deliver the service to the end user.
Each step is designed to be fully automated, requiring minimal technical intervention from the user
while guaranteeing a reliable and high-quality output.
The service delivery pipeline unfolds as follows:
Step 1: Registration and authentication. The user creates an account on the platform using their
email address. During registration, they complete a brief intent profile by answering two guided
questions about their project type and analytical goal. This profile is stored and used throughout the
session to personalize all subsequent steps.
Step 2: Data upload. The user uploads their data file in CSV or Excel format through a drag-
and-drop interface. The system immediately performs a silent background scan and displays a
data passport summarizing the file structure: number of rows, number of columns, column types
detected, proportion of missing values, and sample values. This gives the user immediate confidence
that the system has correctly understood their file before any processing begins.
Step 3: Automatic data cleaning. The system runs a multi-step cleaning pipeline automatically.
It removes exact duplicate rows, fills missing numeric values using the median of each column,
fills missing categorical values using the most frequent value, detects statistical outliers using the
standard deviation method and flags them for the userвҖҷs review, standardizes date formats across all
detected date columns, and removes columns where more than 70% of values are absent as these
provide no analytical value. Each operation is logged in real time with a plain-language description
of what was done and why.
Step 4: Cleaning report. Upon completion of the cleaning pipeline, the system presents a struc-
tured cleaning report summarizing every modification made to the original dataset. The user can
download the cleaned version of their file at this stage. The report is written entirely in non-technical
language so that a user with no data science background can fully understand what changed and why.
Step 5: Intelligent analysis suggestions. The system sends the data profile and the userвҖҷs intent
profile to an AI model via a single API call. The AI returns a list of three to five specific analyses that
are relevant and technically feasible given the structure of the uploaded dataset. These suggestions
are presented as clickable cards written in plain business language, such as revenue by product
category or three-month sales prediction. The raw data never leaves the userвҖҷs device during this
stepвҖ”only the statistical profile is transmitted.
Step 6: Analysis execution. The user selects one or more suggested analyses. The system executes
the corresponding pre-built analysis module, which may involve generating interactive charts using
Plotly, running a regression or classification model using Scikit-learn, or performing a clustering
operation using KMeans. Where machine learning models are involved, the system automatically
tests multiple algorithms and parameter combinations using cross-validation and selects the best-
performing configuration without any input from the user. Results are displayed as interactive
visualizations accompanied by a plain-language summary of findings.
Step 7: Report generation and script export. The user can download a PDF report containing all
visualizations, the cleaning summary, and the analysis results formatted for presentation or sharing.
Additionally, the platform generates a fully documented Python script that reproduces every oper-
ation performed during the session, allowing technically inclined users to continue working with
their data independently.
Step 8: Data deletion. Upon the end of the session or explicit logout, all uploaded data and inter-
mediate files are permanently deleted from the server. No user data is retained beyond the active
session. This step is communicated clearly to the user as a trust and privacy guarantee, which is
particularly important in the Algerian market where data sharing hesitancy remains a significant
adoption barrier.

4.2 Infrastructure and Supply
In the context of a digital platform, the concept of supply refers to the technical infrastructure and
third-party services on which the platform depends to function. Our infrastructure strategy is de-
signed to minimize costs during the development and prototype phases while allowing seamless
scaling as the user base grows.
Primary hosting infrastructure: During the prototype phase, the platform is built using React for
the frontend interface and FastAPI for the Python data processing backend. The frontend is hosted
on V ercel, which offers completely free hosting for React applications with automatic deployment
directly from GitHub and no server configuration required. The backend is hosted on Render, which
offers a free tier for Python web services and similarly deploys automatically from GitHub with no
infrastructure management needed. This split architecture eliminates hosting costs entirely during
the prototype and first year of operation while producing a result that looks and behaves like a real
commercial web application rather than a development prototype. As the platform grows beyond
the prototype stage and reaches a paying user base, the backend hosting will migrate to a more
powerful tier on Render or to a dedicated cloud environment on Amazon Web Services or Google
Cloud Platform, both of which offer startup credit programs that can reduce costs significantly
during the early scaling phase.
Development and collaboration tools: The source code is versioned and collaboratively managed
through GitHub, which also serves as the projectвҖҷs continuous integration pipeline. Project man-
agement and documentation are handled through Notion, allowing the team to maintain a shared
23

--- PAGE 24 ---
knowledge base, meeting logs, and task assignments in one place. User interface design mock-
ups and prototypes are created in Figma before implementation, ensuring consistency between the
intended design and the delivered product.
AI API provider: The platformвҖҷs intelligent analysis suggestion feature relies on a single API
call per session to a large language model providerНҫ either AnthropicвҖҷs Claude API or OpenAIвҖҷs
API. API usage costs are minimal given the low token volume of each call, which transmits only a
structured data profile rather than raw data. During the prototype phase this cost is negligible and
can be covered by free API credits available to students and academic projects.
Payment infrastructure: For monetization of the paid subscription tier, the platform will integrate
Chargily, the leading Algerian payment gateway, which supports local payment methods including
CIB and Dahabia bank cards. This is a critical infrastructure choice given that international payment

Conclusion
The work presented in this memoir demonstrates that the technical and strategic foundations of
DzDataAutomate are sound, and that the platform occupies a meaningful and defensible position in
a market that no existing solution currently serves.
From a technical standpoint, the five-stage pipeline : intent capture, data ingestion, automated clean-
ing, AI-assisted analysis suggestion, and model execution , has been implemented as a functional
prototype and validated against representative Algerian business data. The platform successfully
handles the core operations of the data science workflow without requiring any technical input from
the user, and the locally deployed AI assistant generates contextually relevant, plain-language ana-
lytical suggestions without transmitting raw data to external servers. The architecture is grounded
entirely in open-source components, which ensures that the cost structure remains viable for the
target user segment and that the platform can be maintained and extended without dependency on
proprietary infrastructure.
From a strategic standpoint, the analysis conducted in Chapter 3 confirms that the platform enters
a market with no direct local competitor and a well-defined unmet need. The Algerian SME sec-
tor, the student and researcher community, and the NGO ecosystem collectively represent a large
and growing addressable market for which the cost, complexity, and cloud dependency of interna-
tional AutoML platforms constitute insurmountable barriers. DzDataAutomateвҖҷs combination of
local deployment, French and Arabic language orientation, freemium pricing, and browser-based
accessibility positions it as the natural first choice for this segment, and its privacy-preserving archi-
tecture directly addresses the data sovereignty concerns that represent the primary adoption barrier
in the Algerian digital context.
From a financial standpoint, the projections presented in Chapter 5 confirm that the platform can
reach operational self-sufficiency within its first year of commercial operation under realistic as-
sumptions, with no requirement for debt financing and a working capital structure that is inherently
favorable due to the subscription-before-use payment model. The open-source technology stack
and zero-cost hosting strategy during the prototype phase eliminate the capital barriers that typi-
cally constrain early-stage software ventures.
The prototype presented in Chapter 6 constitutes the first concrete realization of these principles and
demonstrates that the platformвҖҷs design philosophy translates effectively into a navigable, coherent
user experience. The screens implemented cover four of the five pipeline stages and provide the jury
with direct evidence that the platformвҖҷs most distinctive features : the intent-capture interface, the
automated cleaning report, the interactive visualization engine, and the AI strategic insights panel ,
function as described in the preceding chapters.
Several directions for future development follow naturally from the work presented here. The in-
tegration of additional analysis pattern modules ( particularly clustering, anomaly detection, and
time series decomposition ) will broaden the range of analytical needs the platform can address
without requiring changes to the core architecture. The development of an Arabic-language inter-
face layer will remove the final linguistic barrier separating the platform from the full breadth of its
target market. The establishment of formal partnership agreements with university incubators and
selected SME beta partners will accelerate the transition from prototype to commercial product and
generate the early user testimonials that are essential to market credibility.
DzDataAutomate begins as a student project and an academic deliverable. Its ambition, however,
is larger: to demonstrate that advanced data analysis capabilities can be made genuinely accessible
to the organizations and individuals who need them most, in the market where they have been most
systematically overlooked, and to establish a locally developed platform as the reference solution
for data-driven decision-making in Algeria and the broader North African region. The foundations
laid in this memoir constitute the first step toward that objective.
